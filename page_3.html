Student Support Services at UMSI

About CAPS

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS), part of University Health Service (UHS), supports the mental health and well-being of the campus community.

CAPS offers services like individual and group counseling, crisis support, workshops, and more to help students manage challenges and improve their overall well-being.

Embedded CAPS Counselor at UMSI

UMSI students have access to an embedded CAPS counselor, Ashley Evearitt, who provides personalized mental health support. Here’s how to get started:

  1. Email Ashley Evearitt at evearitt@med.umich.edu to express interest in starting therapy.
  2. Complete the secure demographic information form Ashley provides. This form helps tailor the session to your specific concerns and aids in creating a personalized treatment plan.
  3. Schedule an Initial Consultation (IC) appointment, which typically lasts 30-50 minutes. This session focuses on discussing your concerns and beginning to structure your treatment plan.

Services through CAPS are covered by student fees, so there’s no cost to you. Your clinical information is confidential and not shared with UMSI staff unless you provide written consent.

Additional Support through CAPS

  • Crisis Support: If you are experiencing an urgent need, call the CAPS front desk at 734-764-8312 to speak with a Counselor on Duty (10 AM–6 PM, Mon-Thu; 10 AM–5 PM, Fri).
  • After-Hours Support: CAPS provides mental health support during evenings, weekends, and holidays. Call 734-764-8312 and press 0 to speak with a licensed mental health professional.
  • Individual Peer Counseling (IPC): IPC connects UM students with trained peers of similar age, background, or interests for support, active listening, and guidance on personal challenges, transitions, and campus life in a relaxed, judgment-free space. Meeting with an IPC.
  • Wolverine Support Network (WSN): Join a student-led peer support group to connect with others and discuss day-to-day stressors. Learn more at WSN Groups.
  • Stressbusters App: Use the Stressbusters app for relaxation tools, health tips, and campus event updates. More info here: Stressbusters App.
  • Therapy Off-Campus: For long-term therapy or specific specializations, use the Community Provider Database to find local providers.
